The foundational statements ELIXIR-UK derives its objectives, audiences and channels from. Everything in the rest of the comms strategy reads against these.

## Mission

> Supporting UK life science community to build, connect, and sustain world-class digital research infrastructure – promoting standards, shaping policy and amplifying UK voices.

Approved 2025. Replaces the 2019 statement (*"Helping UK life science researchers use biological information effectively, by promoting development of the required infrastructure."*) following the 2024 SIAB review – the shift is from pure infrastructure provision toward people, collaboration and community-building, aligned with the UK 2021 Life Sciences Vision.

## Vision

> A UK research ecosystem where every life-science researcher and technical professional shapes and benefits from a world-leading, collaborative and FAIR digital research infrastructure that is AI-ready.

Approved 2025. The previous strategy didn't articulate the vision as a single sentence.

## Tagline

> Empowering people. Supporting services. Advancing AI-readiness. Sustaining digital research infrastructure.

Approved 2025. ELIXIR (parent) has historically used *"Data for life"*; ELIXIR-UK hadn't held a tagline before.

## Dual identity

For funders (ESFRI, EU, UKRI) ELIXIR-UK presents as a **Research Infrastructure**. In day-to-day work we operate as a **distributed network of member organisations**. Both framings are true; lead with whichever is most useful to the audience.

The mission, vision and tagline above are deliberately written to hold both together while keeping the network dimension visible. For the sector-wide context behind this choice, see [Brand](/brand).

## Cause, impact, actions

A sense-check framework when proposing new activities. If a proposal doesn't connect cleanly to all three, it probably isn't on-mission.

### Cause

The group ELIXIR-UK exists to impact, in the most specific terms:

- **Who:** UK life-science researchers and research technical professionals.
- **What we are trying to change for them:** how they work with life-science data and information.
- **Why specifically the UK:** other organisations (ELIXIR Hub, ISMB, RDA, GOBLET, ECCB) work with the same broad audience. ELIXIR-UK's specific value is adapting activity to UK researchers' needs.

### Impact

What we want to enable for the cause:

- Greater insight into research questions, obtained faster.
- More effective sharing of data, tools, insights and analyses – less effort to share, less effort to find and reuse.
- Reproducibility of own and others' analyses with fewer resources.
- More reliable, robust and sustainable tools.

Financial framing: reduce the cost and time required to create UK economic value from life-science data.

### Actions

How ELIXIR-UK delivers the impact:

- Strengthen interactions between the people delivering UK life-science infrastructure (meetings, online and in-person).
- Raise awareness of ELIXIR-UK Node resources (publicity, contributions to UKRI/RDA/government consultations).
- Grow the consortium to include the key elements of UK life-science information infrastructure.
- Channel funding to consortium members (ELIXIR Commissioned Services, support for external applications).
- Increase diversity and inclusion in the people delivering this infrastructure.
- Engage with external stakeholders (UKRI, ELIXIR, RDA, etc.) to represent UK community needs.

## Values

These are how ELIXIR-UK functions, not what it does. Listed in no priority order – the values balance each other rather than compete.

- **Financial sustainability**
- **Scientific rigour**
- **Collaboration**
- **Inclusiveness**
- **Respect**

Under load (limited time, resources or political capital), when one value is compromised the others get heavier weight.
