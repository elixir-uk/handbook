ELIXIR-UK's voice is the personality behind every word we publish — webstories, social posts, emails, grant copy, event announcements. It doesn't shift based on the channel. (Tone does — see [Voice & tone](/voice-and-tone) for the difference.)

## The voice in one sentence

> _To author. A single sentence the rest of this document derives from. Something like: "Warm, precise, and curious — like a scientist who genuinely wants you to understand." Replace with the line that feels true to ELIXIR-UK._

## Voice pillars

We write with these qualities in tension. None of them is the whole voice — the voice is the way they balance.

### 1. Clear, not simplified

> _To author. The principle. We talk to scientists, students, funders, the curious public — but never down to any of them. We swap jargon for plain English where we can; we keep the technical term when it's the right word and explain it once._

**Why:** _to author — what's the cost when we get this wrong?_

### 2. Warm, not cute

> _To author. Friendly because the people we're talking to are people. But never twee, never "look how relatable we are" — the work is the work._

### 3. Confident, not corporate

> _To author. We know what we're doing and we say it. No hedging, no "we're excited to announce" filler. Direct doesn't mean cold._

### 4. _Add a fourth if there's one missing_

> _To author._

## What we're not

Explicit anti-patterns. Naming what we're not is sometimes clearer than naming what we are.

- **Not:** Corporate-research-marketing. We don't write "leveraging cutting-edge solutions to accelerate innovation."
- **Not:** Academic-passive. We don't write "it was found that..." when we mean "we found that..."

|---|---|
| Webstory | _To author. Probably: warmer, more narrative, more space._ |
