The terms we use, the terms we don't, and the spelling and capitalisation choices we've made so everyone writes them the same way.

## How to use this page

When you're drafting and you're unsure ("is it 'open source' or 'open-source'? Is it 'data set' or 'dataset'?"), this is the page. If the term isn't here and the answer mattered, add it after you decide.

## Words to avoid

> _To author._

- "leverage" — usually means "use"
- "ecosystem" — usually means "thing"
- "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art", "best-in-class" — usually mean nothing
- "exciting" / "excited" — almost always cut-able

## Spelling and capitalisation

> _To author. UK English (we're ELIXIR-**UK**)._

- **British English** — organise, colour, programme. Exception: "program" when referring to software/code.
- **Dataset** (one word) not "data set"
- **Open source** (two words, no hyphen as a noun); **open-source** (hyphenated as an adjective before a noun, e.g. "open-source software")
- **FAIR** — always capitalised, refers to Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable
- **Sentence case** for headings — not Title Case
- _to author — more_

## Acronyms and initialisms

> _To author. The ones we use, and how we introduce them._

- First use: spell out + acronym in parens. e.g. "Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable (FAIR)"
- Subsequent uses: acronym alone
- Exception: very widely-known acronyms (DNA, UK, EU) — don't spell out
